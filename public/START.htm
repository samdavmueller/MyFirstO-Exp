Group version of 'Guess the Urn'

Please read these instructions carefully!

There will be a quiz when klicking 'Done'. Only if you can answer all questions in the quiz correctly, you can proceed to the game. If you fail five times, your HIT will be rejected. Make sure you understand all of the instructions.


In this game, you have to guess the color of an urn together with two automated players in a group.

Just like in the previous game, the urn can be blue or red. Both colors are equally likely. You receive signals that can help you make your decision. All signals you receive contain a color, either red or blue. On average, six out of ten signals contain the same color as the urn they are describing, while four signals show a different color. This time, the game consists of two stages:

In the beginning, each player receives between 0 and 2 signals. No other player has the same signals or knows how many or which signals you received.
In the first part of the game you have to decide whether you want to share a signal with the automated players or hide it from them. If you share the signal, the automated players take it into account when making their decision. If you hide the signal, the automated players cannot take your signals into account when making their vote choice.

The exact probability of receiving 0, 1, or 2 signals is shown in the table below.

Number of signals Probability
0 1%
1 18%
2 81%


The automated players always share all of the signals they receive.

After your decision to share or hide your signal(s), you will see all shared signals (both, from you and the automated players) and you have to make a voting decision on the color of the urn.

The automated players will see all signals that were shared as well. They always both make the same voting decision.
You have information about the players previous decisions for the six urns you have faced in the previous game:

Urn Number Signals Decision
Urn 1 blue, red, red, red, blue, red
Urn 2 blue, blue, red, red, blue
Urn 3 red
Urn 4 red, red, blue, red
Urn 5 blue, red, red, blue, blue, blue

During the game you can use the yellow button above to re-read the information about the automated players' behavior.


In the end all the votes for 'blue' and 'red' are counted and the color with more votes is the group decision.
If the group decision and the true color of the urn match, the possible payoff for the round is 30 ECU (Experimental Currency).
Remember, 10 ECU are equal to $.

The game is played over six rounds. Only two randomly chosen rounds of these six are paid in US$ in the end.
Be aware that the color of the urn might change in each round as you face different urns.
The automated players and their vote decision with respect to signal realizations stay the same through all rounds.

After the game you can earn more money by completing additional tasks.
If you understand the instructions press the 'Done' button to proceed to a quiz about the instructions.
Only if you can answer all questions in the quiz correctly, you can proceed to the game. Make sure you understand all of the instructions.
