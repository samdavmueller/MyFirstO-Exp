Instructions for the 'Guess the Urn'-Game

Please read these instructions carefully!


This game is played by one human player and two automated players.

In each round, you and the automated players have to decide as a group whether an urn is blue or red. You are paid when your group decision matches the true color of the urn. Both colors are equally likely.

In the beginning, each player receives between 0 and 3 signals. No other player has the same signals or knows how many or which signals you received.
The exact probability of receiving 0, 1, 2 or 3 signals is shown in the table below.

Number of signals Probability
0 3%
1 19%
2 44%
3 34%


All signals you receive contain a color, either red or blue. On average, six out of ten signals contain the same color as the urn they are describing, while four signals show a different color. For example, if the urn is blue, there is a % chance that the signal you receive is blue and a % chance that it is red.

In the first part of the game you have to decide whether you want to share an signal with the automated players or hide it from them.

The automated players always share all of their signals.

After your decision to share or hide your signal(s), you will see all shared signals (both, from you and the automated players) and you have to make a voting decision that indicates your belief about the true color of the urn.

The automated players will see all signals that were shared as well. They will then make their voting decision as follows:
1. They count the number of shared signals that show 'red' and multiply it by .
2. Then they count the number of shared signals that show 'blue' and multiply it by .
3. Finally, the automated players compare the numbers they calculated and vote for the color that has the larger number. If both number are the same, a coin toss decides the color they choose.

In the end all the votes for 'blue' and 'red' are counted and the color with more votes is the group decision.
If the group decision on a color and the true color of the urn match, you earn 30 ECU (Experimental Currency).
You can earn additional 10 ECU if your vote decision matched the color of the urn. 10 ECU are equal to $.

First you play three unpaid training rounds.
Then, the game is played over three rounds and earnings are added to your final payoff.
Be aware that the color of the urn might change in each round.

After the game you can earn more money by completing additional tasks. Filling out the final questionnaire gives you an additional payment of 20 ECU ($0.34).
If you understand the instructions press the 'Done' button to proceed to the game.
