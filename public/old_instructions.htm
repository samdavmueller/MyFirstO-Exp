Instructions for the 'Guess the Urn'-Game

Please read these instructions carefully!


In this game, you have to guess the color of an urn.

The urn can be blue or red. Both colors are equally likely. You receive signals that can help you make your decision. All signals you receive contain a color, either red or blue. On average, six out of ten signals contain the same color as the urn they are describing, while four signals show a different color. For example, if the urn is blue, there is a % chance that the signal you receive is blue and a % chance that it is red.

You are paid when your decision matches the true color of the urn.

In the beginning, each player receives between 0 and 2 signals. No other player has the same signals or knows how many or which signals you received.
The exact probability of receiving 0, 1, or 2 signals is shown in the table below.

Number of signals Probability
0 1%
1 18%
2 81%


In the first part of the game you have to decide whether you want to share a signal with the automated players or hide it from them.

The automated players always share all of their signals.

After your decision to share or hide your signal(s), you will see all shared signals (both, from you and the automated players) and you have to make a voting decision that indicates your belief about the true color of the urn.

The automated players will see all signals that were shared as well. They will then make their voting decision as follows:
1. They count the number of shared signals that show 'blue' and multiply it by .
2. Then they count the number of shared signals that show 'red' and multiply it by x, which is a number between 0 and 30.
3. Finally, the automated players compare the numbers they calculated and vote for the color that has the larger number. If both numbers are the same, a coin toss decides the color they choose.

The automated players are identical and made the following choices after seeing the signals given below:

Signals Decision
blue, red, red, red, blue, red
blue, blue, red, red, blue
red
red, red, blue, red
blue, red, red, blue, blue, blue

During the game you can use the yellow button above to re-read the information about the automated players' behavior.


In the end all the votes for 'blue' and 'red' are counted and the color with more votes is the group decision.
If the group decision on a color and the true color of the urn match, the possible payoff for the round is 30 ECU (Experimental Currency).
Additional 10 ECU are added to the payoff if your vote decision matched the color of the urn. 10 ECU are equal to $. Only two randomly chosen rounds are paid in US$ in the end.

First you play three unpaid training rounds.
Then, the game is played over six rounds.
Only two rounds are randomly chosen and their payoffs are added to your final bonus.
Be aware that the color of the urn might change in each round.
The automated players and the x stay the same through all rounds.

After the game you can earn more money by completing additional tasks.
If you understand the instructions press the 'Done' button to proceed to a quiz about the instructions.
